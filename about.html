Brandon Chen

A student researcher interested in the intersection of GNSS, urban navigation, robotics, and transport engineering.

Profile

Learning how cities can navigate better.

I am a rising high-school junior in Hong Kong. My academic interests centre on urban GNSS and navigation—especially the positioning challenges created by dense buildings, multipath, blockage, and complex transport environments.

My wider interests include robotics, intelligent transport, sensor integration, and translating research into real systems. During spring and summer 2026, I am working as an independent researcher at IPN Lab and contributing to the hardware and mechanical components of the Stairio project.

Academic preparation

My IB programme includes Higher Level Mathematics: Analysis and Approaches, Physics, and Economics, with Standard Level Chemistry, Chinese B, and English Language & Literature.

Direction

I hope to continue toward engineering research that combines robust positioning with urban mobility and transportation—work that is mathematically rigorous, experimentally grounded, and useful outside the laboratory.
